Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

The Salmon River, located deep within the Frank Church Wilderness of No Return Nature Preserve, is a place that has captivated many. Those who live near the river, known locally as the River of No Return, often find themselves so enamored with its wild beauty that they decide to stay forever. The surrounding landscape is home to herds of elk and deer, and it is not uncommon to spot bears and bighorn sheep. In addition, majestic ponderosa pines line the river, providing shade on the sandy beaches of the campgrounds and protection from sun or rain.

The Salmon River is one of the longest rivers in the United States that has not been interrupted by dams. Its flow varies with the seasons, being higher, faster and cooler in early summer, and lower, slower and warmer in late summer. Although the river offers exciting rapids throughout the summer, families with children often prefer the calmer, warmer waters of late summer for swimming.

The rapids range from class I to IV.

Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

The Green River, which glides through the canyons in the heart of Utah, flows through some of the most inhospitable regions in existence, until it joins the mighty Colorado River and finally the sea. This river, famous for its towering gray rock cliffs, breathtaking desert scenery and thrilling rapids, offers an 84-mile journey that stretches from Sand Wash to Swaseys Rapid, located near Green River, Utah.

This place is characterized by extraordinary scenery, fascinating geological formations, evidence of both prehistoric and historic human activity, and excellent opportunities for whitewater sports. Desolation Canyon has received National Monument status, a recognition that is due to its uniqueness and deep history.

Desolation is rated a Class II/III and is usually done in 6 days.

Grand Canyon, Arizona

The Grand Canyon is considered the progenitor of all canyons and all river adventures. A visit to the Grand Canyon is an experience that will live long in your memory. Grand Canyon rafting offers a combination of thrilling whitewater and spectacular scenery, providing a once-in-a-lifetime experience. In addition to the thrilling rapids, the canyon is home to secondary canyons and ancient Indian ruins, which can only be reached by river.

The Grand Canyon has its own river rating scale, ranging from I - X, to reflect the diversity of its rapids. Despite this, reference is also made to the standard I - V river rating scale, which is more familiar to most people.

The rapids range from class I to X.
